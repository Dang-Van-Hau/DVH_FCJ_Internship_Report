### Speaker highlights (sessions I documented)

#### 1. Team **The Ballers** — Hackathon experience

![Sharing session — Team The Ballers (Hackathon)](/images/4-EventParticipated/4.1-Event1/sharing-the-ballers.png)

**Speakers:** Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Minh Quan · **Team:** The Ballers (FCAJ Challenger)

**Topic:** Their hackathon journey — building under tight deadlines with humor and honesty about the real cost of shipping fast: fun, bug fixes, fatigue, and sleep loss.

**Key takeaways for me:**

- A hackathon mirrors **real cloud projects**: iterate fast, debug constantly, and ship something working — not a perfect first draft.
- **Team communication** under time pressure is as important as technical skill (task split, sync, and clear ownership).
- The FCAJ Challenger context showed how FCJ encourages **learning by building**, not only by reading documentation.

**AWS relevance:** Fast iteration on AWS (Lambda, Function URL / API Gateway, DynamoDB) requires the same mindset — deploy, test, fix IAM issues, redeploy. Their story aligned with how I approached serverless labs in Week 6 and later the URL Shortener workshop.

#### 2. **Huynh Thai Linh** — Effective Practical Learning Roadmap

![Sharing session — Effective Practical Learning Roadmap](/images/4-EventParticipated/4.1-Event1/sharing-learning-roadmap.png)

**Topic:** A structured **3-phase cloud learning path** for students and interns moving from beginner to production-ready.

| Phase | Focus | Tools / approach |
| ----- | ----- | ---------------- |
| **Phase 1 – Mind & Architecture** | Build architectural thinking first | **AWS Cloud Quest** (gamified Cloud Practitioner skills) |
| **Phase 2 – Code & Fast Testing** | Practice coding and rapid validation | Open-source **Floci** for fast cloud testing |
| **Phase 3 – Real Deployment & Production** | Deploy on real infrastructure | **Real AWS** accounts with billing awareness |

**Key takeaways for me:**

- Do not jump straight to the Console without **architecture mindset** — understand *why* VPC, IAM, and regions matter before clicking.
- **Cloud Quest** strengthens fundamentals (security, networking, compute) in a structured way — useful before Solutions Architect certification.
- **Fast testing** (Floci-style iteration) reduces wasted deploy cycles on AWS.
- **Production phase** means real accounts, real costs, and teardown discipline — exactly what FCJ labs teach with Cloud Credits and Billing Alerts.

**Application:** I mapped this roadmap onto my internship: Weeks 1–4 (fundamentals: IAM, VPC, EC2) → Weeks 5–8 (serverless, IaC, containers) → Weeks 9–12 (EKS, analytics, workshop on real AWS).

---

#### 3. **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** — Why We Always Need Confidence

![Sharing session — Why We Always Need Confidence](/images/4-EventParticipated/4.1-Event1/sharing-confidence.png)

**Topic:** **"Why We Always Need Confidence"** — why self-confidence is essential for careers, campus life, and personal growth in tech.

**Subtitle on slide:** *Why it's a "Must-Have" for Our Careers, Campus Life, and Everything in Between*

**Key takeaways for me:**

- Technical skills alone are not enough — **confidence to ask questions, present ideas, and recover from failure** matters in cloud careers.
- Internship and meetups are safe spaces to practice speaking up before job interviews or client presentations.
- Confidence grows from **small wins**: finishing a lab, debugging an error, or explaining a concept to a peer — not from knowing everything on day one.

**AWS relevance:** Debugging AWS (IAM Deny, Route Table, Lambda timeout) requires persistence and confidence to read docs and try again — themes I later applied in worklog challenges.
