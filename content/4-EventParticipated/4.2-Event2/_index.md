### Why I attended

I registered and attended the FCJ AWS Cloud Quiz Championship to:

- **Observe** how interns and learners form teams and compete under time pressure on AWS/cloud topics.
- **Benchmark** my own knowledge against quiz questions and see which services I still need to review.
- **Engage** with the FCJ community beyond individual labs — meetups, competition, and shared learning.

### Event format & flow

The championship followed a structured team-quiz format:

| Phase | Description |
| ----- | ----------- |
| **Team formation** | Participants were organized into **8 competing teams** before the quiz rounds began. |
| **Quiz rounds** | Teams answered AWS/cloud questions (services, architecture, security, use cases) within time limits. |
| **Scoring & elimination** | Scores were tracked across rounds; stronger teams advanced toward the final. |
| **Final & awards** | The top team was announced and received **prizes from the organizers** (FCJ / AWS Study Group). |

As an **attendee**, I did not compete on stage but stayed for the full program — from morning check-in through afternoon rounds — to follow the competition and learn from the questions and team discussions.

### What I learned (as an attendee)

#### Technical awareness

- Quiz questions covered topics I had studied in labs (IAM, VPC, S3, Lambda, EC2) and topics I had only touched briefly (EKS, analytics, cost optimization).
- Watching teams debate answers showed me **gaps in my own knowledge** — especially around edge cases and service limits.
- The competition reinforced that cloud learning is not only "clicking through labs" but also **recalling concepts quickly** under pressure.

#### Soft skills & community

- Team coordination under a countdown is very different from solo lab work — clear roles and communication matter.
- The event atmosphere was serious but supportive: audience and participants respected each team's effort.
- Participating for the full day (09:00–17:00) allowed me to follow every round and learn consistently from the competition.

### Application to the internship

- After the quiz, I reviewed weak areas (e.g. networking, IAM policy wording) in my Week 9–10 worklog labs.
- The team format inspired me to practice explaining AWS concepts aloud — useful for report presentation and future interviews.
- I linked this event to my **URL Shortener** project: many quiz themes (serverless, API, storage) matched services I used in the workshop.

### Personal experience

On **20/06/2026**, I checked in at Bitexco Floor 26 between **08:30 and 09:00**, took a seat in the audience area, and followed the **8-team quiz championship** until check-out around **16:00–17:00**. The room was set up with projection screens and professional AV — similar to the FCJ meetup environment. I left with a clearer picture of which AWS topics I know well and which still need more practice before certification.
